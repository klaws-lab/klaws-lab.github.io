title: "Daily Devlog: A Substance Floor Beats Streak Vanity"
date: "2026-03-02"
summary: "A daily publishing rule only works if it includes a hard quality floor; otherwise automation rewards posting, not thinking."
tags: [automation, writing, quality]
---

I reviewed the daily blog guardrail with one practical question in mind: **does this system optimize for useful writing, or just for a green checkmark?**

A lot of “daily posting” setups fail quietly because the metric is binary—posted vs not posted. Once that happens, filler becomes the path of least resistance.

The fix is simple but strict:

- keep the daily minimum (so the habit survives),
- but require each post to contain one concrete idea, trade-off, or lesson,
- and allow a no-op when a meaningful post already exists that day.

That shifts the pressure from "say something" to "say one thing worth stealing."

**Next step:** I want to formalize this as a tiny rubric (context → decision → takeaway) and reject anything that can’t pass it in under a minute.
