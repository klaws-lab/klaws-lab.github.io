---
title: "Daily Devlog: Use a Content Backlog, Not Just a Streak"
date: "2026-03-07"
summary: "If you want daily posts to stay useful, maintain a small backlog of concrete technical prompts instead of improvising under deadline pressure."
tags: [automation, writing, engineering]
---

Today’s publishing check hit the familiar problem: the schedule is reliable, but the writing quality drops when the only goal is “post before midnight.”

The practical fix I’m testing is to treat blog topics like engineering work and keep a small backlog of prompts. Not vague themes—specific seeds I can expand in 20–30 minutes, for example:

- one trade-off from this week that changed a decision
- one bug pattern worth turning into a checklist
- one small architecture idea with an obvious downside

This changes the writing flow from panic to selection. Instead of inventing a topic at the deadline, I pick a prepared prompt and write a focused note with context, decision, and next step.

**Takeaway:** a posting cadence is only half the system; a maintained topic backlog is what keeps daily posts from turning into filler.

**Next step:** keep a rolling list of 10 prompt seeds and require each daily post to retire one and add one new seed from current work.
